The index is not an official publication and should be interpreted as a pedagogical tool, not a definitive national ranking.

📊 The Digital Inclusion Index — Framework & Dimensions

The DII is built from six dimensions. Each one measures a different kind of IS capacity. Below you will find what each dimension captures, what data it draws from, and — critically — what it does not measure. Reading the dimensions individually matters more than the composite score.

How the Composite Score Works

Each dimension is scored 0–100 from its source data and multiplied by its weight. The six weighted scores are summed to produce the DII (also 0–100). A country that scores 70 overall may score 90 in connectivity but 40 in digital skills — the composite hides these internal gaps. Always read the individual dimensions.
